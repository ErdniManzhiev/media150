Reflection #2

Nesrine Malik's "With 'AI slop' distorting our reality, the world is sleepwalking into disaster" argues that the use of artificial intelligence in the internet not only changes our image consumption, but also fundamentally reshaping how we perceive our reality. The author shows how a world is divided into 2 parallel image channels: the one that is made of real news, politics, our everyday life, and the other that she calls "AI slop", which is low quality and mass produced AI generated content. And this AI slop goes from harmless cute videos to distorted political propaganda and racism. While some people will find it funny and trivial, Malik that it is dangerous.

AI slop distorts reality rather than just blurring it - that is a claim that author makes. Because of the realism of AI made images we can't trust what we see. For example, Malik writes about the use of AI in politics like rightwing propaganda and how it provides an infinite number of fictional scenarios. These AI generated scenarios allow people to create idealize political images without, and the only limit is the power of AI that is being used. Unlike the traditional propaganda that needs real actors and real events, AI propaganda can make infinite not true victories, enemies, etc. AI makes propaganda more accessible, harder to trace, and the main thing - it distorts this foe people who can't tell the difference.

Malik emphasizes how this AI content spreads through social platforms like WhatsApp, where it often goes unquestioned. There, people trust the ones who sends AI slop. For example, elder person who received it from a friend or family. It makes AI misinformation and distortion very powerful in hands of older people and everyone who can't tell the difference between AI and real events. And even if people doubt the content, it still makes people feel emotions when it aligns with their beliefs. The danger of AI slop is not just technological but also psychological.

Another important argument that author of the reading makes is that AI is structurally conservative. To explain what she means, we need to know that AI trains on the existing human data, and it often have biases. It biased with white supremacy and racism. This bias is not random, it reflex on what AI was trained on. And as a result, AI content can reshape biased fantasies as normal.

However, not all AI slop s a part of political propaganda. Its actually that most of AI content is simply created for profit. For social media platforms, AI is a cheap and effective at keeping and attracting people's attention. Even absurd generated fantasies have comments and likes. Companies want people to keep scrolling so they could make money with little losses.

And, the most warning thing is distortion of our reality so we start to feel numb. The constant flow of AI content overwhelms us. So, we can take it as a warning. The danger of AI slop is not just misinformation, its also slow removal of our ability to distinguish truth from fiction.
